Overall Assessment

This test took approximately 12 hours rather than the estimated 2-3 hours. This extended timeline reflects my focus on comprehensive structure and professional presentation.

I prioritized showing my full understanding of the commercial, technical, and operational requirements, which may have resulted in more detail than strictly necessary. However, this approach reflects how I typically approach client-facing work: thoroughly and systematically.

Task-Specific Reflections

Task #1: Proposal Scoping & Costing

Without detailed knowledge of Bullshark's internal workflows, I found it challenging to balance the project scope within budget while meeting the 30-40% margin target with the given hourly rates and time allocations.

That said, I believe that through effective organization and execution, there are hidden time-saving benefits that could improve these margins in real-world delivery. The pricing structure reflects a balance between competitive positioning and quality delivery—a realistic tension I'd navigate collaboratively with your team.

Task #2: Tender Compliance & Response Framework

This was the most challenging task, partly due to the presentation format and my instinct to provide highly detailed project documentation. I approached it as I would a real tender submission—comprehensive and compliance-focused.

For the technical narrative, I researched relevant evidence and positioned based on available information. With deeper knowledge of Bullshark's specific capabilities and past projects, I would strengthen this positioning with more targeted internal proof points and competitive differentiation.

Task #3: CRM Workflow Logic & Systemisation

This was the most straightforward task for me. I designed the pipeline and automation logic with an outbound marketing and lead generation approach, assuming I'd be managing a team of executives with a formal, detail-oriented workflow style. The structure reflects systematic process management with clear accountability and measurable outcomes.
